Motorized window shades used to be reserved for luxury homes and commercial buildings. Not anymore. Today's rechargeable motors are affordable, whisper-quiet, and integrate seamlessly with the smart home ecosystem you're probably already building.

## How Motorized Shades Work

At its core, a motorized shade replaces the manual chain or cord with a small electric motor hidden inside the roller tube. You control it via remote, smartphone app, or voice assistant. The shades raise and lower smoothly and silently — no more yanking on chains or dealing with tangled cords.

Our motorized shades use **Somfy motors**, the industry standard trusted by hotels, offices, and luxury residences worldwide. The rechargeable version plugs in via USB-C for charging and lasts 6-12 months per charge depending on daily usage.

## Smart Home Integration

This is where motorized shades really shine. With our optional Smart Hub ($185), your shades connect to your home WiFi and integrate with:

**Amazon Alexa** — "Alexa, close the bedroom shades." It's that simple. You can also create routines like "Goodnight" that close all shades, lock doors, and turn off lights in one command.

**Google Home** — Same voice control, plus integration with Google's automation routines. Set your shades to open gradually with your morning alarm.

**Apple HomeKit** — Control shades from your iPhone, iPad, or Apple Watch. Create scenes and automations through the Home app.

**Schedules** — Set your shades to open at sunrise and close at sunset automatically. No hub required for basic timer functions — the remote handles simple schedules on its own.

## The Cord-Free Safety Advantage

Beyond convenience, motorized shades eliminate dangling cords entirely. This is a significant safety upgrade for homes with children and pets. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has documented hundreds of cord-related incidents. Going motorized removes the risk completely.

## Battery Life and Charging

The rechargeable Somfy motor charges via a standard USB cable. A full charge takes about 4 hours and powers 6-12 months of typical use (2-3 operations per day). When the battery gets low, the app sends a notification. You charge it in place — no need to remove the shade.

For windows that get heavy daily use (like a home office with multiple adjustments throughout the day), consider the hardwired option. It connects to a standard outlet and never needs charging.
