Specialty shape windows are architectural showpieces — until you need to cover them. Standard blinds and curtains simply don't work for triangular, trapezoidal, hexagonal, or arched windows. That's where custom-shaped roller shades come in.

## The Specialty Shape Challenge

Non-rectangular windows create three problems that standard window treatments can't solve. First, off-the-shelf products only come in rectangles. A triangular window gets a rectangular shade that either leaves gaps or covers too much wall. Second, the angles make mounting tricky — standard brackets don't align with angled frames. Third, the aesthetics suffer when you try to force a rectangular solution onto a non-rectangular window.

Custom-shaped roller shades are manufactured to match your window's exact geometry. A triangular shade for a triangular window. A trapezoidal shade for a trapezoidal window. The fabric follows the shape, the mounting hardware is engineered for the angles, and the result looks intentional rather than improvised.

## Shapes We Manufacture

We produce custom shades in six specialty shapes:

**Triangle** — Common in A-frame homes, cathedral ceilings, and gable windows. Available in right triangle and isosceles configurations.

**Trapezoid** — Found in modern architecture, dormer windows, and clerestory designs. The angled top or bottom follows your window's exact pitch.

**Hexagon** — A striking geometric window that's impossible to cover with standard treatments. Our hexagonal shades mount cleanly within the frame.

**Pentagon** — Five-sided windows appear in bay window configurations and modern home designs.

**Arch Top** — Semi-circular windows and arched transoms. The shade follows the curve precisely.

**Quarter Circle** — Corner windows and fan-shaped transoms. These require precise manufacturing to match the radius.

## How Custom Shapes Are Made

Each specialty shade starts with your exact measurements. Unlike rectangular shades where we just need width and height, specialty shapes require additional dimensions — angles, radii, or multiple side measurements depending on the geometry.

Our [shade builder](/builder) walks you through the specific measurements needed for each shape. You'll see a diagram showing exactly where to measure and how to record each dimension.

The fabric is then precision-cut using CNC equipment that follows your shape to within 1/16 of an inch. The roller mechanism is modified to accommodate the non-rectangular profile, and custom brackets are prepared for your specific mounting angle.

## Fabric Considerations for Specialty Shapes

Most fabrics in our collection work for specialty shapes, but there are a few considerations. Heavily textured fabrics may not conform as cleanly to tight angles. For triangles with acute angles (less than 30 degrees), we recommend smoother fabric textures for the best appearance.

For specialty shape windows that are purely decorative (like a fixed triangular window above a door), light filtering fabrics are popular because they let in soft light while providing a finished look. For specialty shapes in bedrooms or media rooms, blackout fabrics work just as well in custom shapes as they do in rectangles.
