Ordering Custom Shades for New Construction: Timing and Tips is one of those decisions that feels cosmetic—until you live with glare, heat gain, privacy issues, or a shade that simply doesn’t fit.
In this guide, I’ll walk you through the specs that matter (measurements, fabrics, lift systems, and hardware), realistic price ranges, and the common mistakes that cause returns.
Primary keywords we’ll cover: custom shades new build, window treatments new construction.

## Why timing matters for new construction window treatments

New builds move fast at the end: paint touch-ups, flooring, trim, and final inspections can all change clearances around your window.
Ordering too early is the #1 cause of fit issues, especially when casing thickness or stool projections change late in the schedule.
The goal is to order as soon as window sizes are final—but after the surfaces that affect bracket clearance are installed.

## The ideal measurement window (and what must be finished first)

Best practice: measure after windows are installed, drywall is finished, and trim is installed (or at least planned exactly).
If you’re doing inside-mount, confirm you have adequate depth. Many roller shade brackets need about 2 inches of depth for a clean fit; deeper cassettes can need more.
If you’re doing outside-mount, confirm header/trim alignment so the shade sits level across multiple windows—this matters in open-concept great rooms.

## Budgeting: realistic price ranges for a whole-house order

For a typical home with 12–18 windows, custom manual roller shades often total roughly $1440–$7560 depending on sizes and fabric.
Motorization is where budgets swing: add about $250–$650 per shade for quality motors and remotes (more for hardwired or smart integration).
If your builder offers a window-treatment package, compare it against direct-to-consumer pricing and verify what’s included (measure, install, valances/cassettes, remotes).

## Coordinating with trades: power, casing, and mounting surfaces

If you want hardwired motors, you need power planned before drywall. Decide early and coordinate with the electrician.
For inside-mount shades in wood jambs, confirm the jamb is square and deep enough; for drywall returns, confirm there’s solid backing for anchors.
Ask for consistent head heights on adjacent windows so your shades align. Small differences are very noticeable once shades are installed.

## Choosing fabrics by room function (not just aesthetics)

Bedrooms: blackout or room-darkening, ideally with outside-mount coverage or side channels if you’re sensitive to light.
Living areas: light-filtering or solar screen for glare control without killing daylight.
Bathrooms: moisture-resistant materials and easy-clean fabrics.

## FAQ


### How do I measure for ordering custom shades for new construction: timing and tips?

Measure width and height in three places, use the smallest width for inside-mount, and always double-check depth and obstructions like handles or trim.

### Should I choose light filtering or blackout?

Choose light filtering for living spaces where you want daylight and glare control; choose blackout for bedrooms, nurseries, media rooms, or any room where sleep or presentations matter.

### What is a realistic budget per window?

For most custom roller-style shades, plan roughly $120–$420 per window, with motorization adding about $250–$650 when desired.

### Can I order samples first?

Yes—samples are the fastest way to confirm color, texture, and light behavior in your home. Order them from /swatches and test morning vs. afternoon light.

## Pro measurement checklist (print this before you measure)

Write down each window name (for example: ‘Primary Bedroom – Left’) and record width at the top/middle/bottom and height at left/center/right.
Note obstructions: cranks, alarms, trim projections, tile returns, or anything that could interfere with a bracket.
If you’re outside-mounting, sketch where the brackets will land and confirm you have solid material for anchors.
Finally, verify your control side (left/right) based on furniture placement and outlet location if you plan motorization.
Order free fabric samples from /swatches before you commit—color and openness can look different in your light.
