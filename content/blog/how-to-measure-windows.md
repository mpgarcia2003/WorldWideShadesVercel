Getting accurate measurements is the single most important step in ordering custom shades. A shade that's even 1/4 inch off can look wrong or fail to block light properly. The good news: measuring is straightforward when you follow a simple process.

## Tools You'll Need

Before you start, grab a steel tape measure (not a cloth one), a pencil, and our [printable measurement worksheet](/guides/WWS-Window-Measuring-Guide.pdf). A steel tape measure gives you accurate readings down to 1/16 of an inch, which matters for inside mount installations.

## Inside Mount Measurements

Inside mount shades sit within the window frame for a clean, built-in look. You'll need three measurements:

**Width:** Measure the inside of the window frame at three points — top, middle, and bottom. Record the **narrowest** measurement. We'll manufacture the shade to this exact width with a small deduction for clearance.

**Height:** Measure from the top of the frame opening to the sill at three points — left, center, and right. Record the **longest** measurement.

**Depth:** Measure the depth of your window frame from front to back. You need a minimum of 1.5 inches for a standard roller shade, or 3 inches if you want the shade fully recessed. Our cassette valance requires 3.5 inches of depth.

## Outside Mount Measurements

Outside mount shades attach to the wall or molding above and around the window. This is ideal for shallow frames, better light blocking, or when you want the shade to cover a larger area.

**Width:** Measure the area you want to cover. We recommend adding 3 inches on each side of the window frame (6 inches total) for optimal light blocking.

**Height:** Measure from where the brackets will mount down to where you want the shade to end. Add 3 inches above the frame and extend at least to the sill.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't round.** If your measurement is 36 and 3/8 inches, record exactly that. Our builder lets you enter fractions for precision.

**Don't assume windows are the same size.** Even windows that look identical can vary by 1/4 to 1/2 inch. Measure each window independently.

**Don't measure old shades.** Always measure the window itself, not an existing shade that may have been manufactured to different specifications.

**Don't forget the obstructions.** Check for handles, locks, or cranks that might interfere with the shade operation. Note these when ordering.
