Spring is the perfect time to rethink your windows. After months of being sealed up for winter, your home is ready for a fresh look. Here are five ways to transform your space without a full renovation.

## 1. Swap Heavy Curtains for Clean Roller Shades

If your windows still have the floor-length drapes that came with the house, it's time for an upgrade. Roller shades give you a clean, modern look that makes rooms feel bigger and brighter. They're also significantly easier to clean — no more dragging curtains to the dry cleaner.

The visual difference is dramatic. Heavy curtains absorb light and make rooms feel closed-in. Roller shades sit flush with the window, opening up wall space and letting architecture speak for itself.

## 2. Go Light Filtering for Spring Brightness

Winter often calls for heavy, room-darkening window treatments. Spring is the opposite — you want that golden morning light streaming in. Light filtering roller shades diffuse harsh direct sunlight into a warm, even glow while still protecting your furniture and floors from UV damage.

Our light filtering collection includes 479 fabrics in everything from crisp whites to warm neutrals to bold colors. There's a shade for every room and every style.

## 3. Add Motorization to Existing Windows

Already have roller shades you love? You don't need to replace them. Our Somfy rechargeable motors can be retrofitted to most standard roller shades. It's a 15-minute DIY upgrade that gives you smartphone control, voice assistant integration, and automated schedules.

Imagine waking up to shades that open gradually with the sunrise. Or coming home to a house that's already adjusted for the evening. That's the motorization upgrade.

## 4. Try a Bold Fabric in One Room

Playing it safe with neutrals in every room? Spring is the time to experiment. Pick one room — maybe a powder bathroom, home office, or breakfast nook — and go bold. A deep navy, forest green, or terracotta shade can completely transform a space.

Our [free swatch program](/swatches) lets you test up to 10 fabrics before committing. Hold them up to your window at different times of day to see how the color shifts with natural light.
